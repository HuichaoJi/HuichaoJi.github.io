Hi, I'm Huichao Ji (冀慧超), a postgraduate student (Master's degree expected in June 2022) in Department of Psychology at Sun Yat-sen University. I work with Dr. Xiaowei Ding. Before working with Dr. Ding, I did my undergraduate work with Dr. Jing Samantha Pan.

This fall, I will be a Ph.D. student at Yale working with Dr. Brian Scholl. Soooo excited!!

We live in a social world with a highly intelligent cognitive system. I'm interested in how does human mind use capacity-limited cognitive systems to process unlimited information in the world by focusing on perception, attention, and working memory. For social information processing, we have investigated how do we perceive (Ji & Pan, 2019; Shao et al., 2020; Sun et al., 2021) and attend (Ji et al., 2020) to others' actions and social interactions.

Currently, I'm working on some more fundamental aspects of cognition including the following:
(1) What is the manipulation unit of visual working memory?
(2) How does our memory system dispose of information with different priorities?
(3) How do we use memory to guide attention in a world full of uncertainty?
